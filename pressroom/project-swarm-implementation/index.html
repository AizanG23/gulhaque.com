PRESS RELEASE

Groundbreaking advancements in energy and defense infrastructure.

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

JUNE 9, 2025 – JERSEY CITY, NJ

CEO Aizan Gul Announces Groundbreaking Implementation of Project Swarm in Clark County, Nevada

Gul and Metzen Solutions today announced the commencement of the groundbreaking implementation phase for Project Swarm in Clark County, Nevada, with initial constructions slated to begin on August 15th. This strategic deployment marks a significant milestone in establishing a new paradigm for energy independence and national security within the United States, leveraging Nevada's unique geographical advantages for optimal performance.

Project Swarm is a comprehensive, AI-powered energy grid and autonomous defense ecosystem, developed and engineered entirely in-house by Gul and Metzen Solutions. This fully proprietary platform integrates advanced clean energy generation with next-generation security, ensuring unparalleled reliability and resilience without reliance on external suppliers or third-party technologies. This commitment to self-reliance guarantees 100% energy independence, providing a stable and secure power source for the nation.

The initial phase will see the establishment of a state-of-the-art facility planned to encompass up to 200,000 acres in Clark County. This expansive hub will integrate Gul and Metzen's proprietary Qadira Solar Array System, featuring AI-reactive panels that self-align for peak efficiency, alongside the advanced ARCOS 1 Small Modular Reactors (SMRs). These SMRs, also developed entirely in-house, will provide stable, baseload power, ensuring a continuous, clean, and significantly more efficient energy supply for the region and beyond. The facility is designed for maximum energy output and minimal environmental footprint, with panels elevated to allow for natural wildlife migration.

At the core of Project Swarm's operational integrity is its AI-driven grid optimization and robust cybersecurity, known as Sentinel Core. This system provides self-healing capabilities and military-grade firewalls, safeguarding the energy infrastructure from both physical and cyber threats. Furthermore, Gul and Metzen's autonomous "The Hive" drone network, also developed entirely in-house, will provide continuous aerial monitoring, inspection, and rapid response capabilities, securing the entire energy complex and ensuring operational continuity under any conditions. These multi-role drones are also being developed for critical applications in Homeland Security, offering advanced surveillance, rapid response, and non-lethal intervention capabilities. Gul and Metzen Solutions is committed to donating these cutting-edge drones to various departments and agencies, including California Wildlife and LA Fire, for critical uses such as wildfire prevention, search and rescue operations, and law enforcement support to prevent casualties and provide safer alternatives in high-risk situations.

Project Swarm is poised to deliver not only enhanced security and reliability but also significantly more affordable energy for families and businesses across the United States. By leveraging advanced, efficient, and self-reliant technologies, Gul and Metzen Solutions aims to reduce long-term energy costs and foster economic stability.

Gul and Metzen Solutions is working closely with key U.S. federal bodies, including the Department of Energy (DOE) and the White House, to ensure Project Swarm's seamless integration into the national infrastructure and its alignment with strategic energy and security objectives. Terms are being finalized with investors to secure the strategic initial funding necessary to accelerate this critical national initiative.
